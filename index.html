我是杭州电子科技大学自动化（人工智能）学院控制工程专业的硕士研究生，研究方向为多模态时序信号建模与解码。作为学生作者参与多篇 EEG 解码相关论文（1 篇已发表 + 3 篇在审），核心创新如下（点击可跳转至论文页面）：

  • PCMAN · Applied Soft Computing（IF≈6.6 · JCR Q1 · 中科院计算机 2区 TOP；第二作者（导师一作），已发表）：提出渐进式跨模态对齐网络，以”物理 → 语义 → 任务”三层渐进机制融合 EEG 与 fNIRS——用滑动窗口建模血流动力学延迟、以 fNIRS 空间注意力增强 EEG，系统性缓解两种信号的时空异质性。
  • PhysioSync · IEEE T-AFFC（IF≈9.8 · JCR Q1 · 中科院计算机 1区 TOP；第二作者（导师一作），大修已返修）：面向连续情绪回归，在多模态融合之前先做 HRF 式神经血管对齐（可学习双伽马核），并把对齐残差转化为可靠性门控抑制不可靠的血流动力学证据，配合线性复杂度 Mamba 编码长程 EEG。
  • Neuro-SSCL · IEEE JBHI（IF≈6.8 · JCR Q1 · 中科院医学 2区 TOP；第二作者，初审中）：将神经科学先验（容积传导的局部空间相关 + 全局功能连接拓扑）注入半监督对比学习，并以谱自适应多尺度图对比与多域自适应，缓解跨被试 EEG 情绪识别的标签稀缺问题。
  • AG-LRTD · IEEE TETCI（IF≈6.5 · JCR Q1 · 中科院计算机 2区；第三作者，初审中）：提出注意力引导的低秩张量分解，把 MI-EEG 组织为”通道 × 时间 × 频带 × 试次”四阶张量，用卷积注意力引导张量积低秩分解，并以 tubal-rank 约束 + 残差稠密隐式正则兼顾判别力与可解释性。

  1. ASOC
    Progressive Cross-Modal Attention Network for Brain–Computer Interface Decoding Using EEG and Functional Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (fNIRS)
    Yunyuan Gao, Xiaoqi Luo, Zhengnan Zhang, and 2 more authors
    Applied Soft Computing. IF≈6.6 · JCR Q1 · 中科院计算机 2区 TOP. Second author (advisor Yunyuan Gao 高云园 first author). Framework: PCMAN. , Jun 2026
    Abs DOI Bib HTML PDF

    Electroencephalography (EEG) offers millisecond-level temporal resolution but limited spatial localization, whereas functional near-infrared spectroscopy (fNIRS) provides better spatial resolution but shows a hemodynamic delay of several seconds. A hybrid EEG-fNIRS brain-computer interface fuses the complementary strengths of both modalities but faces challenges from intrinsic spatiotemporal heterogeneity between electrophysiological and hemodynamic signals. To address this, this study proposes a Progressive Cross-Modal Alignment Network (PCMAN), which achieves deep EEG-fNIRS fusion and alignment through a three-layer progressive mechanism: physical alignment (a Spatiotemporal correlation Dynamic Fusion framework, SDF), semantic alignment (a Multi-Dimensional Feature Enhancement Attention network, MDFEA), and task alignment (a Cross-modal Adaptive Decoding Network, CADN). On three cognitive tasks–mental arithmetic (MA), word generation (WG), and motor imagery (MI)–PCMAN achieved accuracies of 88.18%, 81.93%, and 72.94%, outperforming state-of-the-art hybrid BCI methods.

  2. Under Review
    Neuro-SSCL: A Neuro-Aware Semi-Supervised Contrastive Learning for Cross-Subject EEG Emotion Recognition
    Jiangwen Lu, Xiaoqi Luo, Weixiang Gao, and 2 more authors
    IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics (Under Review). IF≈6.8 · JCR Q1 · 中科院医学 2区 TOP · Under Review (Initial Submission). Second author (corresponding author: advisor Yunyuan Gao). Framework: Neuro-SSCL. , 2026
    Abs Bib

    Electroencephalography (EEG) has been widely used in emotion recognition, yet the scarcity of labeled samples remains a major obstacle to its broader application. Existing semi-supervised EEG-based emotion recognition methods mainly rely on data-driven distribution alignment strategies and have not fully explored the neurophysiological priors underlying affective neural activity. To address this issue, we propose a neuro-aware semi-supervised contrastive learning framework, termed Neuro-SSCL. It incorporates neuroscience-derived inductive biases into model design and optimization. Neurophysiological topology-aware soft contrastive learning (NT-SCL) explicitly integrates local spatial correlations induced by volume conduction and global functional connectivity topology into contrastive learning, while spectrum-adaptive guided multi-scale graph contrastive learning (SA-MGCL) enhances emotion-relevant spectral components and imposes multi-scale graph contrastive constraints. A semi-supervised multi-domain adaptation strategy further mitigates distribution shifts across labeled/unlabeled source and target domains via adversarial alignment. On SEED, SEED-IV and SEED-V under a semi-supervised cross-subject leave-one-subject-out protocol, Neuro-SSCL consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods under different incomplete-label conditions.

  3. Under Review
    Neurovascular Alignment and Reliability-Aware Fusion for Continuous EEG–fNIRS Emotion Regression
    Yunyuan Gao, Xiaoqi Luo, Zhengnan Zhang, and 3 more authors
    IEEE Transactions on Affective Computing (Under Review, Major Revision R1). IF≈9.8 · JCR Q1 · 中科院计算机 1区 TOP · Under Review (Major Revision, R1). Second author (advisor Yunyuan Gao 高云园 first author). Framework: PhysioSync. , 2026
    Abs Bib

    Continuous affect tracking is central to closed-loop affective brain–computer interfaces and adaptive human–computer interaction. Continuous EEG–fNIRS regression is well suited to this setting because EEG captures rapid neural dynamics and fNIRS provides complementary hemodynamic information, but direct fusion often underperforms EEG-only baselines when neurovascular coupling (NVC) introduces subject- and region-dependent delays. PhysioSync addresses this by performing HRF-like temporal alignment and residual-aware reliability control before multimodal fusion. A learnable dual-gamma kernel maps EEG-derived drive to the fNIRS timeline through finite-window causal convolution, and the resulting residuals modulate the contribution of hemodynamic evidence during fusion. A linear-complexity Mamba encoder provides long-context EEG representation. On the 32-subject REFED within-subject 3-fold benchmark, PhysioSync reduces Avg MAE by 6.1% relative to the best fusion baseline and by 7.5% relative to EEG-only, while showing reduced error growth in 1–3 s trajectory forecasting.
